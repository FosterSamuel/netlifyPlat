Software Engineering Intern

2019, Google

Student Assistant

For a summer at Cal Poly Pomona I prepared and helped run a week-long cybersecurity camp for high schoolers. The attendees learned security principles and practiced on Raspberry Pis and LEGO Mindstorms. They made their own ethernet cables. Linux was taught.

Each day I walked the group of 60+ students to the Los Olivos dining hall. Sitting and talking with them it was clear: young techinical talent abounds. They were sharp and determined to build.

Freelance Developer

2015

I built and designed websites for five years.

Technically, it pushed me. I learned about the finicky side of CSS. About website optimization. About JavaScript.

And how none of that was the focus. When you build for people, websites need to be organized, fast, and accessible. Everything else is icing on the cake and not something the client needs to be involuntarily briefed on.

I learned how to talk in and translate between the two layers.

Math Tutor

2014

For two years I tutored SAT math, algebra, geometry, and calculus.

Every student had different struggles. Most commonly there was a lack of foundational skills: arithmetic, equations, and pattern matching. The worst offense was that no one had explained why math is important, specifically, to each student.

We took the time needed to build mathematical confidence, which opened them to learning new math tailored to their interests.
